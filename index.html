The December Pivot

Cyber Week, AI Week, and DefenseTech Week were scheduled for June 2025. Then the 12-Day War (Operation Rising Lion) came. We decided not to cancel; instead, we moved them to December.

We chose to keep going. Hosting these global events sent a clear message: the Israeli cyber and AI ecosystem remains open for science, innovation, and international dialogue.

Global Focus

Our flagship conferences draw global attention. Following DefenseTech Week, over 30 leading international media outlets featured the event, including The Wall Street Journal, NPR, and Agence France-Presse. The extensive coverage highlighted Israel's rapid evolution into a premier defense tech nation. Reports celebrated a record breaking year with over 1 billion dollars invested in Israeli startups. Reporters explored hot topics discussed at the summit like AI in modern warfare, defending against drone swarms, and breakthrough innovations like the Iron Beam laser system. This widespread media footprint highlights our role at the forefront of global security.

Thought Leadership

Public Engagement & Media Visibility

Prof. Isaac Ben-Israel, the leader of the ICRC and the Yuval Ne'eman Workshop for Science, Technology and Security, maintained a profound media presence throughout the year. As a Former Director of the Israel Space Agency; former head of the Administration for Research and Development of Weapons and Technological Infrastructure (MAFAT) at the Ministry of Defense, his commentary on advanced air defense, high-energy laser systems, space technologies, and drone threats provided crucial insight and national resilience during the ongoing security challenges.
