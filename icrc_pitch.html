Our Foundation

A World-Class Research Ecosystem

The Blavatnik ICRC is a living intellectual hub, a year-round nexus where the world's leading minds converge to tackle the most urgent challenges in cybersecurity and AI.

Vibrant Research Community

With over 330 affiliated researchers, the ICRC sustains a continuous cycle of academic exchange: seminars, working groups, and cross-disciplinary collaborations that run year-round. Ideas flow between computer science, engineering, law, policy, and the social sciences, creating the fertile ground where breakthroughs emerge.

Premier Research Corpus

At the center's core is a distinguished cohort of globally recognized researchers, experts in network security, AI safety, cryptography, privacy, and critical infrastructure protection. These leading scholars are the driving force behind the ICRC's prestige, attracting top international collaborators and shaping the field's most pressing frontiers.

The First Call for Cyber Strategy

The Blavatnik ICRC is the first name that comes to mind when governments, international organizations, and industry leaders need authoritative guidance on cybersecurity strategy. Through our regular conferences, we bring together top policymakers, provoke thought, shape strategies, and influence policies worldwide.

We are transparent about our work. All research results and outputs are published.

National Trust

Trusted with the Nation's Most Critical Assets

When it comes to defending critical national infrastructure and educating the next generation of cyber warriors, the ICRC stands at the center.

Atlas Academy: Forging Cyber Defenders

The Israel Ministry of Defense (IMOD) and the IDF built the Atlas Academy to educate the nation's top military technology staff. The ICRC provides the academic backbone for this program, directly connecting frontline defense education with cutting-edge university research. In 2025 alone, over 5 Cyber Atlas cohorts were delivered, deepening the bond between national defense and academic excellence.

National Electricity Grid Protection

Having won a national competition led by the Ministry of Energy and the Israel National Cyber Directorate (INCD), our researchers are now developing advanced anomaly detection systems for the National Electricity Production System, in partnership with the National Energy Lab and Ben-Gurion University. This is cyber defense at the infrastructure level.

Real-World Results

2025 Breakthroughs & Global Impact

This year, ICRC researchers discovered vulnerabilities protecting billions of users, won national security competitions, and placed research at the world's most elite academic venues.

CRITICAL GLOBAL DISCOVERY

The “MadeYouReset” HTTP/2 Vulnerability

In collaboration with Imperva, ICRC researchers uncovered a major protocol-level flaw in HTTP/2, affecting over 100 global vendors and potentially billions of internet users. The team led a coordinated responsible disclosure process with the US CERT, ensuring patches reached critical infrastructure before the vulnerability could be exploited.

The discovery drew massive global media coverage from SecurityWeek, HackerNews, and leading cybersecurity outlets, proving the ICRC neutralizes threats at the protocol level.

The December Pivot

Cyber Week, AI Week, and DefenseTech Week were scheduled for June 2025. Then the 12-Day War (Operation Rising Lion) came. We decided not to cancel; instead, we moved them to December.

We chose to keep going. Hosting these global events sent a clear message: the Israeli cyber and AI ecosystem remains open for science, innovation, and international dialogue.

We hosted high-level policy sessions on cutting-edge frontiers, from critical infrastructure and AI in health to cybersecurity in the quantum era, digital identity, and cybersecurity in photovoltaic systems.
